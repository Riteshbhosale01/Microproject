Origin of Lavni

Lavni (also spelled Lavani) is a vibrant and expressive traditional dance and music form from the Indian state of Maharashtra. Known for its powerful rhythm, bold expressions, and captivating performances, Lavni has been a significant part of Marathi folk culture for centuries.

Music and Instruments

Lavni music is lively and energetic. The main instruments used are:

  • Dhol (traditional drum)
  • Dholaki
  • Manjeera (Cymbals)
  • Tuntuna
  • Harmonium

Traditional Costumes

Lavni Costume (In Short): Nauvari saree – 9-yard drape for easy movement Jewelry – Nath, necklaces, bangles, earrings, waist belt Hair & Makeup – Bun with flowers, bold eye makeup, bindi Anklets (Ghungroo) – For rhythmic sound during dance Bright, traditional, and designed for expressive, energetic performances.

Festivals and Occasions

Lavni is mainly performed during: Festivals – like Ganesh Chaturthi and local village fairs Tamasha Shows – folk theatre performances in villages and towns Cultural Events – state-level cultural programs and competitions Weddings & Social Gatherings – sometimes as entertainment Religious Celebrations – devotional Lavnis for temples or spiritual events
