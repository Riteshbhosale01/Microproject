Bihu – The Traditional Folk Dance of Maharashtra

Origin of Bihu

Bihu is the main festival of Assam, celebrating Assamese culture and agriculture. There are three types: Rongali Bihu (April): Assamese New Year; marks sowing season; celebrated with dances and songs. Kongali Bihu (October): Mid-crop season; solemn prayers for protection of crops. Bhogali Bihu (January): Harvest festival; feasting, bonfires, and games.

Music and Instruments

Bihu music is lively and energetic. The main instruments used are:

  • Dhol (traditional drum)
  • Pepa
  • Taal (Cymbals)
  • Gogona
  • Xutuli

Traditional Costumes

During Bihu, traditional Assamese attire is worn, which is colorful and vibrant, especially for dances and celebrations: Women Mekhela Sador: The traditional two-piece silk or cotton outfit. Blouse (Riha): Worn under the mekhela sador. Ornaments: Necklaces, bangles, earrings, and sometimes floral hair accessories. Men Dhoti: Traditional lower garment. Kurta or Shirt: Worn on the upper body. Gamosa: A traditional handwoven cloth often draped around the neck or used as a sash.

Festivals and Occasions

Bihu dance is performed during the Bihu festivals of Assam, mainly to celebrate the agricultural seasons: Rongali Bihu (Bohag Bihu) – mid-April Marks the Assamese New Year and the sowing season. Most energetic and widely performed Bihu dance. Kongali Bihu (Kati Bihu) – mid-October A more solemn festival; dance is less common, mostly prayers and rituals. Bhogali Bihu (Magh Bihu) – mid-January Harvest festival; dance is performed during community feasts and bonfires.
